We wanted to share with you an exciting opportunity for your students to get involved with bullying prevention, as well as a chance for exciting prizes and recognition! Students with Solutions is a new contest focused on engaging K-12th grade students to address bullying through videos, art and writing. It’s simple to enter:

  1. WATCH
    Watch the new, animated video series created from student drawings and writings. These videos provide insight into how painful bullying can feel, but also that kids want it to stop and want to be part of the solution. Each video has accompanied classroom discussion questions. Videos can be found at PACER.org/bullying/students-with-solutions.

  2. CREATE
    Encourage students to write poetry or stories, create a drawing or video, or design a graphic responding to the themes portrayed in the videos. This includes sharing what it means to be together against bullying or ways to help other feel that they belong.

  3. SUBMIT
    Send your images, writings, or videos by email to bullying411@PACER.org or mail to PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center, 8161 Normandale Blvd., Bloomington, MN 55437. Entries will be accepted through January 31, 2017.

RECEIVE AWARDS & RECOGNITION
Four classrooms will be awarded LilGadgets headphones for every student along with a pizza party (value up to $500) for their entire class. Other prizes include Unity medals, colorful rolls of Duck Tape, and books from Simon and Schuster.
