Dance to make a statement against bullying on Oct. 26!

Dance, unite—and make a statement against bullying—by joining PACER’s Unity Dance Day on Wednesday, Oct. 26.  Join people in schools, malls or homes around the world as they dance and unite against bullying with PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center.

Hear the song and learn the dance steps created especially for Unity Dance Day. The song, “You Can’t Take That Away From Me,” reminds students that that personality and character are more important than popularity and outward appearances. Nashville songwriter Tim Akers created the song.

“Music and dance is a powerful way to reach kids and teens about the bullying prevention message,” says Julie Hertzog, director of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center. “Let’s do that on Unity Dance Day!”

Promote the event

  • At your school - Tell a teacher, guidance counselor, or principal about Unity Dance Day and ask if the school can participate.
  • Online, in person, and in your community - Share and “attend” the UNITY DANCE DAY Facebook Event

How Your School or Organization Can Participate:

  • Determine a location. For example: the school lunchroom, the hallway, a classroom, the football field or an assembly.
  • Establish a time. Will it take place in the morning, during lunch, during half-time, or after school?
  • Consider who will participate. Will it be a phy ed class, soccer team, the band, the teachers, or the entire school?
  • Consider what is needed to play the song. For example: a sound system, the school’s PA system, or speakers and an MP3 player?

What Next?

  • Learn the steps
  • Practice, practice, practice!
  • Dance—and make a difference!
  • Post your videos to UNITY DANCE DAY Facebook Event
