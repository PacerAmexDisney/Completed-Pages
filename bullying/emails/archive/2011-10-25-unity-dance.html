Dance to make a statement against bullying on Oct. 26!

Show off your dance moves and support bullying prevention on PACER’s Unity Dance Day, Wed., Oct. 26.

Join people in schools, malls or homes around the world as they dance and unite against bullying with PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center.

“Music and dance is a powerful way to reach kids and teens about the bullying prevention message,” says Julie Hertzog, director of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center. “Let’s do that on Unity Dance Day!”

Will you dance?

  • Share and “attend” the UNITY DANCE DAY Facebook Event
  • Hear the song and learn the dance steps created especially for Unity Dance Day. The song, “You Can’t Take That Away From Me,” created by Nashville songwriter Tim Akers, reminds students that that personality and character are more important than popularity and outward appearances.

    -or-

  • Dance to a song of your choice

Beyond the dance; Ideas include

  • Post your videos to UNITY DANCE DAY Facebook Event
  • Setting up a table to sign "The End of Bullying Begins With Me" petition
  • Post a UNITY banner in the hallway for everyone to sign. Purchase a blank 4' x 8' piece of cardstock at a local print or copy store. Write the word UNITY on it in color markers. Provide color markers for people to sign their name and write a statement about why they believe in supporting each other.
