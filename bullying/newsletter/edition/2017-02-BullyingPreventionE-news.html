Students with Solutions awards announced

Thank you to all the students across the country who submitted drawings, poems, and videos for the first Students With Solutions contest!

PACER’s celebrity panel of judges reviewed all entries and selected four schools to receive a classroom set of headphones and pizza party. Special thanks to Lil Gadgets for this generous donation.

The four awarded schools were:

  • Elementary school in NJ

New video, ‘Teen Talk on Cyberbullying,’ released

“Teen Talk on Cyberbullying,” an unscripted video designed to provide authentic insight and perspective from peers on issues related to cyberbullying, is now available online. The video is part of Students with Solutions, an initiative that engages students to address bullying through videos, art, and writing.

This is the second contest offered through Students with Solutions. This round is presented in partnership with Clarisonic. After viewing and discussing the video, students can share their poetry or stories, create a drawing, film a short video, or design a graphic that are then eligible for awards, including Clarisonic's MIA 1 sonic facial cleansing device.

Watch the video and learn how to get students involved.

Unity Awards nominations open

The Unity Awards honor and celebrate students and community members who have gone above and beyond to help address bullying. Everyone is invited to nominate an individual or group for one of four national awards.

This event is sponsored by the National Bullying Prevention Center’s youth advisory board, The Faces of Change.

Erin Barlow is now available as
National Ambassador and motivational speaker

Erin Barlow, a Theater major at UCLA, is available to speak to middle and high school students around the United States. It is Barlow’s mission to let all know they are not alone.

“If you have been bullied, please know that everyone deserves to be safe, respected and valued,” Barlow said at PACER’s Annual Benefit in April 2016. “Never let others determine your self-worth. Striving for happiness and getting help was the best decision I made. I didn’t give up on myself and neither should you.”
