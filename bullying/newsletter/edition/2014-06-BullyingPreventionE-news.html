Inspiring Students

For her 11th birthday party, Anistyn thought big…and kind! Anistyn held a “Be a Friend, Bring a Friend, Make a Friend” party, and invited everyone in her middle school for music, dancing, and food – all created by kids and teens. Anistyn remembers her mom telling her that for every mean person, there are 10 more who are nice, so Anistyn made the theme of her party, “When we stand together, no one stands alone.” Anistyn writes about a new friend who has a disability,  “No one was laughing at Estelle or making fun of her when she got on stage and sang along. We had fun helping her and being nice to her. This was one of my favorite parts of the entire night, because I realized how fun it is to be friends with people who are different.” To top it all off, Anistyn gave up her birthday presents this year in order to donate more than $1,300 to PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center. Safe and Supportive Minnesota Schools

Governor Mark Dayton signed the Safe and Supportive Minnesota Schools Act into law on April 9, 2014. Now the important collaboration between students, educators, administrators, and parents begins as schools develop safer learning environments for every child in the state. Julie Hertzog, Director of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center, said, “Bullying has negatively impacted too many students for too long. Now that this law is in place, educators will have resources to address the behavior and students and families will know that they have the right to be safe at school.” Read more
