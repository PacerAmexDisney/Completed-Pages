What Will You Do for PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month?

During October, raise awareness of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month by reaching out to your school or community, or by making a personal commitment to prevent bullying. Big events, small acts of kindness – they all contribute to a powerful movement to show targets of bullying that they are not alone. Everyone can do something, somewhere, somehow. Maybe you can organize a Unity Day event at your school or attend a Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying in your community. Maybe you’ll make a commitment to invite a student who looks lonely to sit with you at lunch, or to say something kind to each of your classmates. Whatever you do to take part in National Bullying Prevention Month, you will be helping to build a kinder and safer community for everyone.  Learn more.

U.S. Department of Education Releases New Bullying Prevention Guidance

On Aug. 20, 2013, the U.S. Department of Education released a new ‘Dear Colleague’ letter outlining school districts’ responsibilities to address bullying of students with disabilities. The new guidance states that bullying of students with disabilities that prevents them from receiving meaningful educational benefit is considered a denial of their right to a free appropriate public education (FAPE). Read the letter and additional resources on effective bullying prevention practices.

NEW! By Students, For Students: Teens create effective toolkits for bullying prevention

When it comes to bullying prevention, students may be the real experts. Several talented young people have created toolkits to accompany their artistic endeavors in order to inspire, educate, and activate their peers. Here’s an example of a toolkit:

“Stronger”
Jetset Getset, a teen country music group from Indiana, created a toolkit around their song “Stronger,” written to help young people who are feeling discouraged. The writer, Jennifer Eliason, says that  "The teenage years are a time when self-esteem can take a beating. We wanted to write a song about how you can rise above the challenges and the experience can make you stronger." Learn more.

Everyone’s Contribution Matters

You can make a difference. Schools, organizations, parents, and students each play a role in addressing bullying prevention. Every contribution, no matter how small, makes a difference. You could host a community event, speak up for someone who is being bullied, or donate to PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center – anything that says you recognize bullying as a problem and you want to do something about it. Here’s what Trevin Hunte of “The Voice” is doing:
Trevin Hunte from NBC’s The Voice Season 3 Team Ceelo is speaking out about bullying prevention on behalf of PACER. “I personally know because I have experienced bullying,” he says. “That is why I support bullying campaigns every chance I can, because I don’t want kids to feel like they are alone, because they are not the only ones who have gone through it.”
