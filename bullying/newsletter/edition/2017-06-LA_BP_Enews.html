Unity Awards, “Faces of Change, Los Angeles”

Marveon Mabon, an eighth grader at Ralph Bunche Middle School in Compton, CA, and the “Mentors and Protectors” of Burton Tech High School in Los Angeles are receiving the first Faces of Change awards given in the Los Angeles area. Nominated by the LA Youth Advisory Board of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center, this award celebrates “those who have gone above and beyond to prevent bullying and make our communities a kinder, more inclusive place.” Congratulations to Marveon and MAPs! The National Unity Awards and the Faces of Change awards were presented on May 25 at the Bloomington Civic Plaza in Minneapolis, MN.

Delegation from Africa and Haiti Hears about PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center Efforts

On April 21, L.A. staff member Judy French met with a delegation of 12 community advocates from 10 African nations and Haiti in Santa Monica. Sponsored by the State Department, this delegation’s focus was on human and civil rights. They heard a presentation on bullying prevention followed by questions on raising awareness about bullying and increasing community involvement.

Cyberbullying: New Resources for Students, Teens and Parents

Cyberbullying continues to be a major concern for students all around the world. PACER Center and PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center have created resources that will help you navigate the world of social media and online interactions, including videos with teens talking about their experiences and advice and resources on how to stay safe online.

Save the Date: Unity Day is October 25

Be united for KINDNESS, ACCEPTANCE, and INCLUSION on the last Wednesday of National Bullying Prevention Month (otherwise known as OCTOBER)! Make sure you mark your calendar for Unity Day, 10/25 and remember to wear orange.
