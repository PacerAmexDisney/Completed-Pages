October is National Bullying Prevention Month

Join the movement! National Bullying Prevention Month is a campaign founded in 2006 by PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center. The campaign is held during the month of October and is a time when communities can unite to raise awareness of bullying prevention through events, activities, outreach, and education. PACER offers a variety of resources to use during the month and throughout the year to inspire, educate, and involve others to join the movement and prevent bullying in their communities. Make it ORANGE and make it end! What are your true colors when it comes to bullying? If you care about safe and supportive schools and communities, make your color ORANGE on Unity Day. That’s the day everyone can come together – in schools, communities, and online – and send one large ORANGE message of support, hope, and unity to show that we are together against bullying and united for kindness, acceptance, and inclusion. Orange on Unity Day: CustomInk 5th Annual “Be Good To Each Other” T-shirt campaign

The 2015 official Unity Day T-shirt will be available for online orders, through our partner CustomInk and its fifth annual “Be Good to Each Other” campaign, starting Tuesday, September 8th. CustomInk’s Be Good to Each Other campaign, encourages students and others to take a stand for kindness, acceptance and inclusion by designing and wearing custom bullying prevention t-shirts. The 2014 campaign was a tremendous success — 100% of profits from the sales of more than 10,000 T-shirts were donated to PACER. We’re proud to partner with CustomInk as we continue to encourage communities nationwide to join together against bullying.

Hold a Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying in your Community!

Join Friends of PACER for the 6th Annual Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying on Saturday, October 3rd at Mt. Normandale Lake in Bloomington, MN. This family fun event features a 4-mile chip-timed run and 2-mile walk or roll, and offers an opportunity for adults and students to help raise awareness and funds to support bullying prevention. Register online for this event here.

Communities nationwide are invited to hold a Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying in October during PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month or on another date during the year. A Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying is a family-friendly event held by schools, businesses, and communities to show their commitment to keeping students safe at school, in the community, and online. PACER provides a free toolkit, which includes tips, ideas and resources, to plan your event. Does your school, community or nonprofit have an event planned for National Bullying Prevention Month? You Can Sit With Us – An Inspiring Fundraising Story

While talking about kindness during class, 13 girls in the 8th grade discussed bullying and ways they could make an impact. As a group, the girls created T-shirts with the slogan, “You can sit with us,” and a peace sign on the back. Each girl shared a word that described kindness, such as ‘kind,’ ‘beautiful,’ and ‘brave,’ which was placed within the peace sign. The girls are now selling the shirts at their school and hope to see every student wearing this message of kindness. Read more >>>

New Resource – Bullying 101: Guide for Middle and High School Students

PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center is excited to introduce a new publication, “Bullying 101: Guide for Middle and High School Students.” This visual, age-appropriate 14-page guide has easy-to-understand information about bullying. The guide provides the basics for talking with students about what bullying is and isn’t, the roles of students, and tips on what students can do to address bullying situations. Read more >>>

Ask Carmen – Kids Against Bullying

Kids often feel confused about bullying behaviors. “Ask Carmen,” an interactive feature on PACER’s Kids Against Bullying website, is here to help! Carmen provides advice for students who are experiencing bullying, witnessing bullying, or even demonstrating bullying behavior.  One question Carmen has received is, “Why do kids have to bully so much?” Students and parents can read what Carmen has said to other kids, and ask her their own question!
