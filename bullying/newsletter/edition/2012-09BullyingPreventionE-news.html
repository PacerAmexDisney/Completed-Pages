Be Involved in October During PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month

The End of Bullying Begins with Me: that’s the message during PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month. October is a time when communities can unite nationwide to raise awareness of bullying prevention through events, activities, outreach, and education. Resources from PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center make it easy to take action. Be involved, and make plans to wear orange Oct. 10 on Unity Day, when people across America will show support for students who have been bullied.

Make Plans to Wear Orange Oct. 10 on Unity Day

Mark your calendars now for Unity Day on Wednesday, Oct. 10, 2012, during PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month. On Unity Day, people across America – including celebrities like Ellen DeGeneres – wear orange and link together in schools, communities, and online to send a meaningful message of support for those who have been bullied. Watch for news coming soon on how you can order the “official” orange T-shirt for Unity Day through our partner CustomInk. ‘Attend’ and ‘Share’ the Unity Day Facebook Event with family and friends so everyone can ‘Make it Orange and Make it End!’ this October. Tell the World About Your Local Bullying Prevention Event

Are you planning an event during PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month in October? Big or small, be sure to tell us about your activity so we can let the world know. Kylie Morgan’s Commitment to the Cause Inspires a $10,000 Donation

Teens across America are working hard to support bullying prevention, including 17-year-old Kylie Morgan of Newcastle, Okla. Kylie wrote the song “Phoebe” about a young girl who took her own life after being bullied. Producers of the reality TV show “Opening Act” discovered the song on You Tube and invited Kylie to perform with country music superstar Jason Aldean. For Kylie’s efforts, Covergirl cosmetics donated $10,000 to PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center on her behalf.  Are you doing something original to contribute to the cause? Tell us about your cool fundraising ideas (e-mail Bullying411@PACER.org) or show your support and donate now.

PACER’s Student Action Plan Helps Resolve Bullying Situations

If you are involved in a bullying situation you need to take action, whether you are the target, a witness, or the person who bullies. The Student Action Plan, created by PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center, is a powerful tool that will help you develop a strategy to change what’s happening to you or someone else. Students, parents, and educators can use it as a guide to create a sound, logical plan that will help resolve a specific bullying situation. Learn more

Texas High School Speaks Out Against Bullying in a Very Big Way

They say everything is bigger in Texas, including the way students speak out against bullying. More than 1,000 teens at Cypress Ranch High School near Houston came together to create, film, sing, and perform in an amazing “LibDub” video – a type of music video that is filmed in one continuous shot. The CyRanch creation has made a big splash on You Tube and was even parodied on the television show “South Park.”  Check it out
