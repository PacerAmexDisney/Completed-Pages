October is PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month

Join the movement! National Bullying Prevention Month was founded in 2006 by PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center. The campaign is held during the month of October when communities can unite to raise awareness of bullying prevention through events, activities, outreach, and education. PACER offers a variety of resources to use during the month and throughout the year to inspire, educate, and involve others to join the movement and prevent bullying in their communities. Read more

Hold a Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying in Your Community

Join the Friends of PACER for the 6th Annual Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying on Saturday, Oct. 3 at Mt. Normandale Lake in Bloomington, Minn. This fun family event features a 4-mile run (chip-timed) and 2-mile walk or roll, and offers an opportunity for adults and students to raise awareness and funds to support PACER’s bullying prevention programs in Minnesota. Register online today.

Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying events are held across the country, including one being organized by 8th grade student Spencer Amsellem on Oct. 3 in Tallahassee, Fla. Spencer was bullied throughout elementary and middle school, and has gone from a target to an activist who is working to bring awareness to the painful issue of bullying. Spencer is donating all proceeds raised at the Tallahassee Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying to PACER's National Bullying Prevention Center.

Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying is a family-friendly event that can be held by schools, businesses, and communities to show their commitment to keeping students safe at school, in the community, and online. PACER provides a free toolkit with tips, ideas and resources to plan the event. Organizers can share information about a local Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying on PACER’s Community Events calendar.

PACER opens bullying prevention office in Los Angeles

PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center now has a branch office in Los Angeles to better serve the needs of schools and communities in Southern California. “People have been encouraging us to do more in Los Angeles,” said PACER Executive Director Paula Goldberg. “We are very excited about the possibilities.” PACER supporters Michael and Hayley Miller hosted a large event Sept. 26 at their home in Bel Air, Calif., to officially launch the new center and the start of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month in October.

What Parents Should Know About Bullying

Bullying can be an isolating experience, not only for the kids involved but for their parents, too. Fortunately, there is help available. PACER offers a free, interactive online guide that is easy to navigate and provides a comprehensive overview for parents to learn what they can do to address and prevent bullying. Topics covered include the definition of bullying, laws and policy, how to help your child, working with the school, and mobile and online safety. Special thanks to Hopeline from Verizon for partnering with PACER on this important resource for parents. Review the guide

Duck® brand Helps Students Stick Together Against Bullying

To help students learn how to prevent bullying in their communities, Duck® brand is partnering with PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center to spread the word with its “Stick Together™” program. Through this unique awareness-building initiative, Duck® brand is sharing a positive message with students about taking a stand against bullying. Duck® brand is hitting the road with its custom, bright green, 31-foot Duck® Bus for the annual “Rolls Across America” Tour, which is visiting 25 schools from August through November, reaching approximately 10,000 students along the way. Duck® brand has also launched a limited edition “Stick Together Against Bullying” print tape and is donating 10 cents from the purchase of each roll to PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center with a maximum donation of $20,000.
