BULLYING PREVENTION E-NEWS – April 2012

Plan a National Bullying Prevention Month Event Where You Live

Communities across the country will join with PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center on Oct. 6, 2012, by supporting ‘Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying.’ Individuals, schools, businesses, and organizations are urged to get involved in this and other events during PACER’s Sixth Annual National Bullying Prevention Month in October. You can organize a ‘Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying’ in your community by using PACER’s free toolkit.  Read more

FBI Director Honors PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center

PACER Center and Paula Goldberg, one of PACER’s founders and its executive director, were honored by FBI Director Robert Mueller III in Washington, D.C., on March 16. Mueller presented Goldberg with the prestigious Director’s Community Leadership Award in recognition of PACER’s national bullying prevention efforts. Julie Hertzog, director of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center, also attended the ceremony. The award is presented to individuals and organizations whose achievements in crime prevention and educational programs have made a positive difference in their communities. More

PACER Partners With “Bully” Movie to Raise Awareness

America is waking up to the harsh realities of bullying thanks to PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center and the powerful new documentary “Bully.” The “New York Times” review says the film documents the emergence of a movement and a “shift in consciousness of the kind that occurs when isolated, oppressed individuals discover that they are not alone.” As a partner in the Bully Project, PACER is providing educators, students, families, and individuals with the tools to address bullying. The critically acclaimed film has sparked a national discussion on the issue and its website offers PACER’s bullying prevention resources for children with disabilities.

Teens Drive Bullying Prevention Movement in Their Communities

Teens are taking a lead role in the bullying prevention movement in their communities by making the decision to stand up, protect others, and show their support for this important cause. One way to do that is to become involved in the political process. The voice of Minnesota students was heard Feb. 11 at the State Capitol in St. Paul as more than 100 teens wearing orange rallied to support stronger bullying prevention legislation. Students Use Their Creativity to Support the Cause

Students everywhere are raising money for bullying prevention in unique and creative ways. When Josh in Arkansas invited friends to his 10th birthday party, he asked them not to bring gifts. Instead, he requested they make a $5 donation to PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center. “Josh was able to raise $155 at the party with a matching donation from my husband and I,” his mom wrote. “We hope this helps in your efforts to make life easier for the millions of kids who face bullying every day.” Tell us about your cool fundraising ideas (email Bullying411@PACER.org) or show your support and donate now.
