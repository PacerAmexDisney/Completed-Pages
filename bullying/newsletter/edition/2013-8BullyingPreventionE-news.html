PACER’s Eighth National Bullying Prevention Month Set for October 2013

PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month continues to grow as millions of students and individuals will unite in October to say, “The End of Bullying Begins With Me!” People will wear orange on Unity Day – Wednesday, Oct. 9 – to “Make it Orange, Make it End!” Students across the country will take action, along with schools and communities to share the importance of addressing bullying.

Families, Friends will Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying at Events Nationwide

This October, children, teens, families, and other caring adults will participate in Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying events across the country during PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month. Take part in the flagship event in Bloomington, Minn., on Oct. 5 (presented by American Dairy Queen) or hold your own Run, Walk, Roll event in your community. PACER offers a tool kit to help you get started in bringing the fun, excitement, and energy of a Run, Walk, Roll event to your community! Learn more

NEW PACER Video: “You're Not Alone”

It doesn’t always require words to tell the story of bullying and its impact on students everywhere. Sometimes silence speaks volumes, and a new video produced for PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center relays powerful messages about bullying without uttering a single word. “You’re Not Alone” features a diverse group of students who share the realities of bullying along with a message of hope for their peers. NEW PACER Resource Book: “Beyond Sticks and Stones”

PACER’s newest resource for parents wishing to take action against bullying is an important book called “Beyond Sticks and Stones: How to Help Your Child Address Bullying.” This book offers bullying prevention strategies for children at home, in school, and online. It contains the latest information and practical tools that can help parents and others take action against bullying. The book was developed to help parents protect their child or someone they know from being bullied, and to help parents be proactive in their community in addressing bullying. Throughout the book, there are valuable comments from children and teens that illustrate the face of bullying today. Copies of “Beyond Sticks and Stones” are available for $10 each, with discounted prices for orders of 10 or more.  To order, call (952) 838-9000 or (800) 537-2237 and order item BP-7.

Schools Taking Action: Citrus Grove Elementary Takes a Stand

When Shawna Hixon and Lana Barros from Citrus Grove Elementary in Palm City, Fla., learned how many students at their school were worried about bullying, they decided to take action. They developed an innovative program, which is designed to creatively engage students to take action against bullying. The program encompasses everything from presentations to personal statements to school-wide participation. The message to their peers is that you can be friends even if you’re different from each other, and that students should “Be an iStander, not a bystander.” Read more about the program.

Share Your Story with Others

“Once when I was in the second grade, a girl in my class formed a group that would make fun of me and my friends. They used to call me names like stupid face, bug eyes, wimp, loser and they used to whisper to each other and say that I was useless and that I was not needed in life. That group didn’t just make fun of me. They made fun of all the girls in my class. When they made fun of me, I used to feel so rotten inside and feel that no one would understand if I told them so I tried so hard to ignore them but it didn’t help. It made it worse. I used to cry sometimes at night and used to hope that it would get better. It did after I told the teacher.”
