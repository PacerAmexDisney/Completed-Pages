BULLYING PREVENTION E-NEWS

“The End of Bullying Begins with Me” During National Bullying Prevention Month

October is PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month and the message to share is “The End of Bullying Begins with Me.” This is a great time to take action to prevent bullying in your school, community, and online. PACER developed the campaign in 2006 and it has grown into a national movement. Thousands of people across the country will unite to raise awareness through events, activities, outreach, and education, and PACER has some practical resources that make it easier to take action. Stay tuned for news about more bullying prevention events in October. 

Wear the “Official” Orange T-shirt of Unity Day on Oct. 10

Make plans to wear orange on Unity Day! On Wednesday, Oct. 10, people everywhere will come together to support those who have been bullied. You can help the cause by purchasing the “Official” Unite Against Bullying T-shirt. PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center is partnering with CustomInk, the “design online” custom T-shirt company, as part of its “Be Good to Each Other” campaign. You can order the official orange shirt, create your own design, or order one designed by a celebrity, and all profits from the campaign support PACER. Be sure to ‘Attend’ and ‘Share’ the Unity Day Facebook Event with family and friends. Learn more

New “Spookley” Teacher Toolkit Introduces Bullying Prevention to Early Learners

PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center is partnering with the publishers of “The Legend of Spookley the Square Pumpkin” to provide the new online toolkit, “Stop Bullying Before It Starts.”  This innovative digital resource (preschool, K-3) provides links to free tools that can be used in the classroom, including discussion guidelines, learning activities, and multi-subject learning examples. It’s a great way to help young children discover how to prevent bullying.  

PACER Makes it Easy to Plan a Petition Signing Event at Your School

Want to involve everyone at your school in raising awareness for bullying prevention? Hold a “The End of Bullying Begins With Me” petition signing event at your school. It’s an excellent activity to mark National Bullying Prevention Month in October. PACER’s free, downloadable toolkit makes it easy to plan and stage the event. Learn more

Use PACER’s Template Letter to Notify the School About Bullying

Every time your child says he or she has been bullied, you need to take action by contacting school staff. PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center has created template letters that parents can use as a guide for writing a letter to their child’s school. These letters contain standard language and “fill in the blank” spaces so the letter can be customized for your child’s situation.  Read more

Make a Statement By Holding a Student Rally

Students across the country are speaking out about the need for bullying prevention, and a rally is an effective way to make sure community leaders will hear your message. PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center now has a free toolkit available to help students organize a rally. It was developed by Audrey B., a high school student who planned and executed a highly successful rally at her state capitol as part of a senior project. The toolkit offers practical, step-by-step instructions that can be used by students, educators, parents, and other concerned community members who want to improve bullying prevention policy in their state, district, or school. Learn how

Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying Events Raise Awareness Nationwide

From Las Vegas, Nev., to Punxsutawney, Pa., from Jonesboro, Ark., to San Diego, Calif., organizations across the country are staging Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying events to raise awareness in their communities. It’s not too late to hold your own event during National Bullying Prevention Month in October. Download PACER’s free toolkit to learn how your community organization or school can participate.
