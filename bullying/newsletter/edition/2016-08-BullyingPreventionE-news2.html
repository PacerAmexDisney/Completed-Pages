The Cat in the Hat has launched a presidential campaign and will represent all that Dr. Seuss believed about children — that they have the intellect, imagination and power to impact our world positively, while also having fun doing so.

The Cat in the Hat wants to show kids that every vote counts and every voice matters. Ways to support Unity Day on Oct. 19

Together against bullying — united for kindness, acceptance, and inclusion. Make it ORANGE and make it end!

Unity Day, Wednesday, Oct. 19, is the day everyone can come together — in schools, communities, and online — and send one large ORANGE message of support, hope, and unity to show that we are together against bullying and united for kindness, acceptance and inclusion. Learn what you can do.

Here are more ideas to mark Unity Day:

  • Build a Unity Tree. Plant a “seed” of unity in schools and communities to create social change, so that bullying is never again viewed as an accepted childhood rite of passage. The Unity Tree is an interactive, creative, and hands-on activity where everyone can participate. Learn more.

  • Share Creative Kids Cards. This year’s winner of PACER’s annual Creative Kids contest, eight-year-old Isabella, created a notecard based on the theme of unity. Cards are available for $10 per box to support PACER programs and would make a great gift for families at the beginning of the school year or for teachers during National Bullying Prevention Month. Order the cards here.

WE WILL Generation

The WE WILL Generation is an exciting initiative developed by PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center that uses an interactive model to build student leadership and encourage a change in culture to address bullying. The WE WILL Generation was designed for schools and other organizations to leverage resources. It is available for free and easily accessible online. It encourages student conversation to educate, inspire, and support peers to address bullying and create kinder and safer schools. Join the WE WILL Generation.

Bullying and harassment of students with disabilities

Did you know that students with disabilities are two to three times more likely to be bullied than their nondisabled peers? This new features includes a Top 10 list, policies for students with disabilities, and information on self and peer advocacy. Learn more about preventing bullying for students with disabilities.

KIDS AGAINST BULLYING puppet show for youth

The KIDS AGAINST BULLYING puppet program features charming life-sized, multicultural puppets who deliver an important message of bullying prevention. Using an engaging, child-friendly approach, the puppet shows educate, entertain, and inspire. It helps students in first through third grades learn ways to respond if they are being bullied, how to help if they see someone else being bullied, the difference between telling and tattling, and, most importantly, that no one ever deserves to be bullied. Learn how to purchase puppets and scripts for your community.
