Hold a Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying in Your Community!

Join Friends of PACER for the 6th Annual Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying on Saturday, Oct. 3 at Mt. Normandale Lake in Bloomington, Minn. This fun family event features a 4-mile run (chip-timed) and 2-mile walk or roll, and offers an opportunity for adults and students to raise awareness and funds to support PACER’s bullying prevention programs in Minnesota. Register online for this great event.

Communities nationwide are invited to hold a Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying in October during PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month or on another date during the year. This family-friendly event is held by schools, businesses, and communities to show their commitment to keeping students safe at school, in the community, and online. PACER provides a free toolkit, which includes tips, ideas and resources, to plan your event. Share information about your local Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying event on PACER’s Community Events calendar.

Notifying the School About Bullying – Using a Template Letter

Parents should contact school staff each time their child informs them that he or she has been bullied. PACER Center has created template letters that parents may use as a guide for writing a letter to their child’s school. These letters contain standard language and “fill in the blank” spaces so the letter can be customized for your child, including specific letters for children with disabilities. Read more >>>

New Guide for Parents: “Helping Your Child”

Bullying can be an isolating experience – not just for the kids involved, but for their parents, too. Fortunately, there is help. PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center has introduced a new guide, “Helping Your Child.” This online resource provides steps for parents on helping their child if they are experiencing bullying, demonstrating bullying behavior, or witnessing bullying. There is also specific information for parents of children with disabilities. Read more >>>

Send Us Your Story or Video

Inspired Fundraising Stories shares how students, organizations, and schools are raising money for bullying prevention by coordinating events or activities.
