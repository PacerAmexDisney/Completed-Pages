National Bullying Prevention Month – October

Join the movement! The End of Bullying Begins with Me: that’s the message during PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month in October. It’s a time when communities can unite nationwide to raise awareness of bullying prevention through events, activities, outreach, and education. Resources from PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center make it easy to take action. Find out how you can participate by visiting our website.

There are many exciting things happening throughout National Bullying Prevention Month, including:

  • Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying Across the Country
    Communities nationwide are invited to hold a Run, Walk, Roll Against Bullying in October. “So many students are in need of support from adults, other students, and community members to address this issue,” said Julie Hertzog, director of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center, based in Minneapolis. “Community-wide efforts like this show students that there are people who really want to help.” The flagship event, hosted by PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center since 2009, will be held in Bloomington, Minn., on Saturday, Sept. 20. Find out more
  • Wear Orange on Unity Day:
    CustomInk Launches its Fourth Annual “Be Good To Each Other” T-shirt Campaign in Support of PACER
    Make plans to wear orange on Unity Day (Wednesday, Oct. 22) in a show of support for those who have been bullied. PACER partner CustomInk has launched its fourth annual Be Good to Each Other bullying prevention campaign in support of Unity Day and PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Month in October. The campaign encourages students, groups, and others to take a stand for kindness and tolerance by designing and wearing custom bullying prevention t-shirts. Official Unity Day T-shirts are available now for $12, and CustomInk donates all profits (at least $9 per shirt!) to PACER. Order by Oct. 6 for guaranteed arrival in time for Unity Day.
  • Show Your Support, Become a Champion Against Bullying
    PACER is proud to partner with schools, groups, organizations, non-profits, and corporations that are committed to addressing bullying in their communities. Sign on as a Champion Against Bullying to show your support! 
  • Wednesday, Oct. 22 is Unity Day – Make it Orange, Make it End!
    What are your true colors when it comes to bullying? If you care about safe and supportive schools and communities make your color ORANGE on Unity Day. That’s the day everyone can come together – in schools, communities, and online – and send one large ORANGE message of support, hope, and unity. Get involved!

PACER’s We Will Generation

Imagine the power of preventing bullying through student-to-student engagement in which students are educated, inspired, and supported to take positive action to connect with their peers. Students play a powerful role in influencing their school and community culture. To help them, PACER is offering the newly redesigned We Will Generation, an innovative way to engage and inspire student leaders to prevent bullying in their schools. Find out more.

Book Club

Storytelling is one of the oldest and most effective forms of education. Stories have the power to teach, to inspire, and to connect. When students read or listen to a story, they benefit from engaging with the experiences of others and can often look at their own experiences from a different perspective. PACER's book selections are designed to encourage a new generation to recognize that bullying can be overcome through education, compassion, and unity. Learn more.

Video Inspired by 12-year-old who is Passionate About Bullying

Because These Kids Are is a 10-minute dramatic film from the heart of 12-year-old actress Izabela Vidovic. While at a park, a young artist sees a bullying situation unfold in front of her and those involved become the muses for her work. When the person bullying suddenly targets her, she surrenders her pictures to him, opening his eyes to the truth behind who he and these kids really are. Watch the video.

Sharing Your Story

"I have bullied people before for what they look like or how smart they are. But I learned bullying just makes others feel sad. At that time I didn't know how it felt to be all alone and to walk into school feeling scared. Also I lost my best friend in 5th grade because we got into a fight and it was all my fault. Then it spread through my school and that's when I felt all alone and I realized how I made others feel. Throughout the rest of the school year I was so stressed out and I was taking my stress out on other kids that didn't do anything. I want to help all the bullies stop and not lose their best friend like I did. I want to be a social worker when I grow up and share my story and help kids get through it. I would really love to change the world."
