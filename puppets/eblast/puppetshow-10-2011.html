PACER’s COUNT ME IN® Puppets want to visit your school!

Now scheduling puppet shows on disability awareness for the 2011-12 school year

PACER Center’s endearing, child-size puppets have been bringing their message of tolerance and inclusion to Twin Cities area schools and organizations since 1979. PACER’s COUNT ME IN® puppet shows, presented by trained volunteers, offer an engaging, child-friendly way to educate children about disabilities and inclusion. The primary goal of the show is to help children understand that people with disabilities share many of the same interests, likes and dislikes that they do. Everyone wants to play, learn, have friends and be included. COUNT ME IN® offers shows for grades 1-4 and for preschool/kindergarten.

Each elementary show for children in grades 1-4:

  • Costs $100
  • Includes skits focusing on blindness, deafness, and a physical disability. If time permits, a fourth skit will be added, focusing on either Down syndrome, autism, or ADHD.
  • Lasts 45-60 minutes, depending upon age of students
  • Has a maximum audience size of 80-100 students per show, depending upon age

Each PRE/K show for ages 4-5:

  • Costs $75
  • Lasts 30-35 minutes
  • Has a maximum audience size of 50 students
