Don’t miss a very special educator!

As a child with Tourette syndrome, Brad Cohen was ridiculed, mocked, and shunned. Today, he is one very special educator and he’ll share his remarkable story at this year’s PACER Symposium.  Cohen is an award-winning teacher, school administrator, motivational speaker and co-author of the book, “Front of the Class: How Tourette Syndrome Made Me the Teacher I Never Had.” His life story has been featured on “Oprah,” CNN, and in a Hallmark TV movie and the “New York Times.”

Brad is one of three outstanding national speakers who will headline the 10th Annual National PACER Symposium on Children and Young Adults with Mental Health and Learning Disabilities. The symposium brings together 1,000 parents and educators for an impactful day that provides outstanding practical information about children’s mental health and learning disabilities, as well as proactive, positive teaching strategies and interventions.

Register now for this outstanding national event

In addition to many excellent workshop presenters, this year’s other keynote speakers are Jodi Sleeper-Triplett and Steven Koppel.

Empowering Youth with ADHD: Coaching Toward Success
Jodi Sleeper-Triplett is a trainer, master coach, mentor, speaker, and author who is considered by her peers to be the foremost expert on coaching youth with ADHD.

Unique technology helps youth with mental health challenges
Steven Koppel  is the founder of MyMoments, a non-profit social enterprise that is pioneering the use of Expressive Digital Imagery (EDI). This new form of self-expression created on smartphones is being used as a helpful tool for youth with mental health challenges at places such as the renowned McLean Hospital in Boston.
