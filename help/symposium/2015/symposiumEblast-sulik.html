Don’t miss Dr. Read Sulik’s popular children’s mental health workshops!

Helping children and young adults with anxiety or depression are two of the most common challenges facing parents and educators today. By popular demand, Dr. Read Sulik returns to the PACER Symposium in 2015 to present two outstanding workshops about anxiety and depression on Tuesday, Aug. 4 in Minneapolis. Reserve your seat today!

Dr. Sulik is among the excellent national presenters who will headline the 10th Annual National PACER Symposium on Children and Young Adults with Mental Health and Learning Disabilities. The symposium brings together 1,000 parents and educators for an impactful day that provides outstanding practical information about children’s mental health and learning disabilities, as well as proactive, positive teaching strategies and interventions.

Register now for this outstanding national event

Dr. Sulik is a renowned child, adolescent, and adult psychiatrist. He is the Chief Integration Officer at PrairieCare, where he leads the organization’s initiatives in integrating behavioral health services into primary care settings, in addition to providing clinical care as the attending child and adolescent psychiatrist in the Maple Grove Partial Hospitalization Program. Dr. Sulik, who has received numerous national honors and awards, lectures across the country on children’s mental health issues. He will present two dynamic workshops:

Anxiety from the Inside Out

Anxiety produces significant physiological concerns in both children and adults. In this interactive workshop for parents and professionals, Dr. Read Sulik will explore the “Fight, Flight and Freeze” response, and how chronic stress, trauma, and anxiety can lead to systemic health problems, and severe emotional and behavioral issues. Using compelling stories and real-world examples, Dr. Sulik will help participants understand anxiety and why it is easily misinterpreted and misdiagnosed.

Understanding Depression and How to Develop Skills Needed to Help Your Child

Parenting a child with mental health challenges can be incredibly difficult at times, but there are skills you can learn to help your child. In this interactive workshop, Dr. Read Sulik will help parents and professionals learn about “The 3s” of Depression — changes in mood, changes in sleep and energy, and changes in thinking — and the most common problems that result. Participants will learn new skills that parents can use to help their child heal, recover, and prevent recurrences of depression.

6 Education Clock Hours Available

The 6 clock hours offered are designed to meet the requirement for Minnesota teachers renewing their teaching license in understanding the key warning signs of early-onset mental illness in children and adolescents.

In addition to Read Sulik and many other excellent workshop presenters, this year’s keynote speakers are Brad Cohen, Jodi Sleeper-Triplett and Steven Koppel.

A very special educator with Tourette syndrome

Brad Cohen is an award-winning teacher, school administrator, motivational speaker, and co-author of the book, “Front of the Class: How Tourette Syndrome Made Me the Teacher I Never Had.” His life story has been on “Oprah,” CNN, in a Hallmark TV movie and the “New York Times.”

Empowering Youth with ADHD: Coaching Toward Success

Jodi Sleeper-Triplett is a trainer, master coach, mentor, speaker, and author who is considered by her peers to be the foremost expert on coaching youth with ADHD.

Unique technology helps youth with mental health challenges

Steven Koppel  is the founder of MyMoments, a non-profit social enterprise that is pioneering the use of Expressive Digital Imagery (EDI). This new form of self-expression created on smartphones is being used as a helpful tool for youth with mental health challenges at places such as the renowned McLean Hospital in Boston.
