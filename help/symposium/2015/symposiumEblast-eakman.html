How much do you know about concussions and how they can impact learning?

When a student suffers a concussion, the injury itself cannot actually be seen but the resulting learning challenges and disabilities are often much more obvious. Don’t miss Dr. Zack Eakman’s outstanding workshop on concussions at the PACER Symposium Tuesday, Aug. 4 in Minneapolis. Dr. Eakman will provide insight into the nature of concussions, signs and symptoms, and information about managing concussion issues. It is a session you don’t want to miss!

Dr. Eakman, the Assistant Director of Diagnostic Services at Groves Academy, is among the excellent national presenters who will headline the 10th Annual National PACER Symposium on Children and Young Adults with Mental Health and Learning Disabilities. The symposium brings together 1,000 parents and educators for an impactful day that provides outstanding practical information about children’s mental health and learning disabilities, as well as proactive, positive teaching strategies and interventions.

Register now for this outstanding national event

In addition to Zach Eakman and many other terrific workshop presenters, this year’s keynote speakers are Brad Cohen, Jodi Sleeper-Triplett and Steven Koppel.

A very special educator with Tourette syndrome

Brad Cohen is an award-winning teacher, school administrator, motivational speaker, and co-author of the book, “Front of the Class: How Tourette Syndrome Made Me the Teacher I Never Had.” His life story has been on “Oprah,” CNN, in a Hallmark TV movie and the “New York Times.”

Empowering Youth with ADHD: Coaching Toward Success

Jodi Sleeper-Triplett is a trainer, master coach, mentor, speaker, and author who is considered by her peers to be the foremost expert on coaching youth with ADHD.

Unique technology helps youth with mental health challenges

Steven Koppel is the founder of MyMoments, a non-profit social enterprise that is pioneering the use of Expressive Digital Imagery (EDI). This new form of self-expression created on smartphones is being used as a helpful tool for youth with mental health challenges at places such as the renowned McLean Hospital in Boston.

6 Education Clock Hours Available

The 6 clock hours offered are designed to meet the requirement for Minnesota teachers renewing their teaching license in understanding the key warning signs of early-onset mental illness in children and adolescents.
