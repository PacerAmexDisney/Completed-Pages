Keynote Speaker - BraVada Garrett-Akinsanya, Ph.D., A Look at Learning Disabilities, Mental Health, and Culture

BraVada Garrett-Akinsanya, Ph.D., is a licensed clinical psychologist and executive director of the African American Child Wellness Institute, a children’s mental health agency, and president of Brakins Consulting and Psychological Services. She works with local schools and agencies that serve immigrant populations, children with special needs, low-income families, homeless youth, and trauma survivors.

 

Register now for this outstanding national event!

In addition to excellent workshop presentations, this year’s other keynote speakers are:

Keeping ADHD Positive: Nurturing the Spirited, Imaginative and Playful Nature

L. Read Sulik, M.D., is a child and adolescent psychiatrist and a pediatrician.  As chief integration officer and executive director of Prairie Care Institute for Care Delivery Innovation, Education, Training and Research, he leads initiatives on behavioral health services.  Dr. Sulik lectures nationally on numerous children’s mental health topics including depression, anxiety, attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), and learning disabilities.

Take It As It Comes: Surviving a Child’s Suicide

Adam Levy is a singer-songwriter (Minnesota-based The Honeydogs), social activist, educator and parent.  The suicide of his son in 2012 has made him active in public conversations about reducing the stigma of mental illness and trying to better understand and prevent suicide. Levy is a sought-after speaker at conferences on mental health issues.
