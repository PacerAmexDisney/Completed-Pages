Keynote Speaker - L. Read Sulik, M.D. on Keeping ADHD Positive

L. Read Sulik, M.D. is a child and adolescent psychiatrist and a pediatrician. As chief integration officer at PrairieCare and executive director of PrairieCare Institute for Care Delivery Innovation, Education, Training and Research, he leads initiatives on behavioral health services. Dr. Sulik lectures nationally on numerous children’s mental health topics including children’s and youth’s depression, anxiety, attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and learning disabilities.

Register now for this outstanding national event!

In addition to excellent workshop presentations, this year’s other keynote speakers are:

Take it as it Comes: Surviving a Child’s Suicide
Adam Levy is a singer-songwriter (MN-based The Honeydogs), social activist, educator and parent. The suicide of his son in 2012 has made him active in public conversations around helping reduce the stigma of mental illness and in trying to better understand and prevent suicide. Adam is a sought-after speaker at conferences about mental health issues.

The High Cost of Invisibility: A Look at Learning Disabilities, Mental Health, and Culture
BraVada Garrett-Akinsanya, Ph.D., is a licensed clinical psychologist and executive director of the African American Child Wellness Institute, a children’s mental health agency dedicated to the research, delivery and coordination of comprehensive wellness strategies for children of African descent. She also serves as the president of Brakins Consulting and Psychological Services, which has the mission of “providing excellent, culturally competent mental health and consultation services that meet the needs of children and adults.”
