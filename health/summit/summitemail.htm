General Summit Information

Who should attend?

Parents of children with special health care needs or disabilities who are interested in being a strong voice to improve outcomes for children with special needs in our community.

Minnesota parents who serve (or would like to serve) on state, county, and local advisory committees such as:

  • Education: Interagency early intervention committees, special education advisory committees, transition and rehabilitation committees
  • Health: Hospital advisory committees, hospital ethics committees, and medical home teams
  • Human Services: Medical assistance, mental health, deaf and hard of hearing services, city and county human services, and advisory councils

A stipend will be available for parents who request it. Accommodations, mileage, and expenses will be provided for parents from greater Minnesota. Overnight accommodations will be provided for participants traveling more than 90 miles. Mileage will be provided for participants traveling more than 70 miles.

Parents as Leaders in Systems Change

Engaged, Energized and Effective

  • Learn how to advocate in organizations, agencies and systems that serve children and families
  • Learn how to influence public policy and improve personal advocacy skills
  • Enhance communication and collaboration skills
  • Learn how to support and mentor family leaders.
  • Network with other parent leaders
  • Learn about cultural competency

About PACER
PACER is a parent center for families of children and young adults with all disabilities. Based on the concept of parents helping parents, PACER provides individual assistance, workshops, publications, and additional resources to help families make decisions about education and other services.

Directions to PACER
From I-494 take the Normandale Boulevard exit south to 84th Street; turn left. Take the next left onto the frontage road. PACER Center is a one-story brick building immediately north of the bridge. Turn right into the parking lot. Enter on the north side of the building.
