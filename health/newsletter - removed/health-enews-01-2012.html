How PACER’s Family-to-Family Health Information Center (F2F HIC) Can Help

If you need information or individual assistance about navigating the health care system, PACER’s F2F HIC  can help. It provides a central source for Minnesota families of children and youth with special health care needs and/or disabilities and the professionals who serve them to obtain support, advocacy, and information about the health care system. PACER promotes family-centered care and family and professional collaboration at all levels of health care. The F2F HIC also has a strong commitment to promote and support the needs of families from racially, culturally, and linguistically diverse communities.

The Center provides information, individual assistance, and workshops to help families and professionals find answers to questions about:

  • Health insurance – both public and private
  • Medical Assistance, TEFRA, parental fees, waivers, Consumer Directed Community Supports (CDCS), and grants
  • Supplemental Security Income (SSI) through Social Security
  • The appeals process for insurance and government assistance programs
  • Accommodating children with medical conditions that require a special diet at school
  • Working effectively with doctors and other health care professionals
  • Family-centered care
  • Coordinating health care services
  • Billing insurance for health related services included in a child’s Individual Education Plan
  • Locating low-cost medical and dental clinics
  • Obtaining prescription assistance
  • Federal health reform
  • Advocating within the health care system
  • Developing health care summaries and plans for emergencies, home, and school
  • Transitioning from pediatric to adult health care

If you have questions or concerns relating to health care, please contact PACER Center and ask for the Family-to-Family Health Information Center by calling (952) 838-9000 or (800) 53-PACER (toll free in MN) or e-mailing health@pacer.org.

Who are Children and Youth with Special Health Care Needs?

Children and youth with special health care needs (CYSHCN), as defined by the Maternal and Child Health Bureau, “includes all children, who have or are at increased risk for, chronic physical, developmental, behavioral, and emotional conditions who also require health and related services of a type or amount beyond that required by children generally.”  

For more information on CYSHCN, see the chartbook published by the Maternal and Child Health Bureau in September 2011, which is based on data from the 2007 National Survey of Children's Health.

A Potential Funding Option for Children with Disabilities and Special Health Care Needs
Author: PACER Center

Did you know that in Minnesota you can have both private and public insurance? Many times, having both is the best way to access all of the services you need for your child. Minnesotans are fortunate to have a buy-in program, which allows families to access Medical Assistance by paying a parental fee. If your family already qualifies for Medical Assistance because your income meets guidelines, there is no fee. This buy-in program is called TEFRA. The MN F2F HIC hosted a webinar with information for families about TEFRA.
