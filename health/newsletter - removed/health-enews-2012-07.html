DHS Launches Online Application - ApplyMN

The Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS) recently launched ApplyMN, an online application for Minnesota Health Care Programs, SNAP (food support), Child Care Assistance, cash assistance, emergency assistance, family planning, and breast and cervical cancer coverage. The application is available at
applymn.dhs.mn.gov, and requires users to create a Human Services Online Account with a username and password. The application does not cover all programs in Minnesota, especially the programs for children with disabilities and/or special health care needs, but can help begin the process. For questions or help with the new online application, call PACER at 952-838-9000 and ask to speak with a health advocate.

Minnesota Youth Have Chance to Impact Health Care Reform

Teen Citizen Solutions is an effort to engage Minnesotans ages 13-19 in a conversation about what’s important to the future of health and health care in Minnesota. Participating youth are holding meetings and having a conversation through an online forum. They will then prepare a report and take public comment on the draft. The results will be presented to and ultimately guide the policy proposals of the Minnesota Health Care Reform Task Force.

Did You Know? SSI Compassionate Allowances List Is Expanding

On August 13, 2012, the Social Security Administration will be expanding the list of conditions that qualify for Compassionate Allowances (CAL). CALs are a way to quickly identify conditions that meet disability standards. This can speed up the process for receiving Supplemental Security Income (SSI) to help support a child or other family member with one of these conditions. Find the list of CAL conditions on socialsecurity.gov.

Prepare for Medical Visits with These Resources

Communication builds good relationships with doctors and other health care providers, and asking questions is an important way to participate in your own or your child’s health care. It can be a challenge to remember your questions when you’re in the office. PACER’s book Working with Doctors: A Parent's Guide to Navigating the Health System was created to help parents become effective advocates for their children in the health care system. It focuses on communication skills with medical professionals, maintaining records, and other important issues. Families in Minnesota can receive a free copy by calling PACER at 952-838-9000. Another resource, the Question Builder tool from the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, can help you choose some common questions, prioritize them, and print them out to help you remember.

Sunscreen, Sunblock, UV-what?

Last year, the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) announced new regulations about sunscreen labels. The changes are meant to reduce confusion when choosing which sunscreen to purchase. They require sunscreen companies to identify if they provide broad spectrum coverage on the front label. Brands also won't be able to say that products are “waterproof.” This summer, however, many sunscreens will still have old labels. Most experts suggest sunscreen with both UVA and UVB (or broad spectrum) protection, that has at least SPF 15. For more about sunscreen, check out this Sunscreen Infographic, and ask your doctor if you have questions.
