PACER SYMPOSIUM ON CHILDREN’S MENTAL HEALTH & LEARNING DISABILITIES AUGUST 15; REGISTRATION NOW OPEN!

Parents, teachers and other education professionals can now register for the 12th Annual PACER National Symposium about Children & Young Adults with Mental Health and Learning Disabilities. It is on Tuesday, Aug. 11, from 8 a.m. to 4 p.m. at the Minneapolis Convention Center. Lunch is included in the $35 admission price. CEU’s are available. This year’s nationally-recognized speakers include:

  • Anne Gearity, PhD, has a mental health practice in Minneapolis, and is a consultant to many school districts. She also teaches at the University of Minnesota. Gearity is a respected speaker and presenter, and her keynote address will focus on anxiety.

  • Gil Noam, PhD, is the founder and director of the PEAR Institute at Harvard University, and is an Associate Professor at Harvard Medical School and McLean Hospital in Boston. His topic is the essential elements that children and adults need in order to thrive, learn and develop.

  • Richard G. Petty, MD, a world-renowned authority on the brain, and accredited specialist in psychiatry, neurology, internal medicine and nutrition. He will speak on Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD).

The 2017 Symposium will again feature a special presentation by members of the Youth Advisory Board on Mental Health; they will share their experiences as teens with mental health needs, and offer strategies and tips on how to support them to reach their highest potential.

PARENT LEADERSHIP SUMMIT ON CHILDREN’S MENTAL HEALTH IS AUGUST 25

It is important for policymakers to consider the perspective of parents and families when they are creating legislation that impacts children with mental health and emotional or behavioral disorders. Parents who participate in PACER’s Parent Leadership Summit on Children’s Mental Health will receive training on state and local mental health issues, and learn how to work with policymakers and participate on policy committees to impact the mental health system of care for children and families. The summit is on Friday, Aug. 19, from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. at PACER Center. Space is limited. Please contact Renelle Nelson or call 952-838-9000 or, toll-free, 800-53-PACER, to learn more about this unique opportunity.

SUICIDE AWARENESS AND SUPPORT FOR TEENS AT THE END OF THE SCHOOL YEAR

For teens with a mental health, emotional or behavioral need, additional pressure can influence an increase in negative and suicidal thinking. The Minnesota Department of Human Services’ suicide hotline is available 24 days per day, seven days per week. Teens can go online to txt4life.org, or text LIFE to 61222.

THE YOUTH VOICE MAKES A DIFFERENCE!

History has documented gains made in services and supports as a result of listening to the family voice.  Recently, the youth voice has gained impetus in helping to design services to be more responsive to needs of children and youth with mental health, emotional or behavioral needs.  Youth Advisory Boards are one excellent way for youth to impact change while gaining leadership and self-advocacy skills.  PACER Center’s Youth Advisory Board on Mental Health works on these skills and helps align them with improving the very systems that give them support.  More information about how PACER’s Youth Board works, and ideas about how to form a Youth Board
