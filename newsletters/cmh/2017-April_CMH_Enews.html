CHILDREN’S MENTAL HEALTH AWARENESS WEEK IS MAY 1-7

The Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMSHA) has designated the first week in May as Mental Health Awareness Week. The week’s theme is “Finding Help, Finding Hope.” Their statement reads: “As national events continue to illuminate the critical need for mental health care reform in this country, we must increase our efforts to educate the nation about the importance of prevention and early identification of mental health challenges. We must also highlight the fact that children are an integral part of a family unit and create an understanding amongst policy leaders and practitioners that healthy families are better equipped to support resilient children.”

Watch PACER Center’s CMH EDB website for special information and resources for parents and others as they support children and youth with mental health challenges.

PARENT WORKSHOPS ON CHILDREN’S MENTAL HEALTH

Beyond Medicine: How your child’s physician can support wellness and recovery for your child with mental health, emotional or behavioral needs

  • Tuesday, April 18
    6:30 pm - 8:30 pm
    PACER Center

Presented by Dr. Richard Peterson and Dr. Barry Garfinkel, experts specializing in child and adolescent psychiatry. When parents of children or youth with mental health, emotional, or behavioral needs go to their child’s doctor for help, often the only expectation is that they will leave with a prescription for medicine. This workshop will inform parents and others how to optimize the visit through advance preparation, learning what role medications may play in treatment, and how to apply that information to increase better outcomes at school, at home, and in the community. Register for this workshop.
