Dear Minnesota Parents,

During the past few months, you heard from PACER Center several times asking for your help as we advocated for children and youth with disabilities at the State Capitol. Thanks to your help, we were able to stop some negative legislation and be successful in moving other initiatives forward:

  • HF 998, which sought to conduct a cost-benefit analysis to evaluate Minnesota’s special education laws that exceed federal requirements, was defeated. We know your calls and emails to your legislators played an important part to stop this bill. PACER will continue to advocate for Minnesota’s historically strong special education system that leads to improved outcomes for students with disabilities.
  • PACER successfully advocated for improved use of assistive technology for students with disabilities. A provision in the omnibus education bill requires the Minnesota Department of Education to conduct a study on implementation of assistive technology that will lead to a report and recommendations for improvements.

It is extremely important that our state senators and representatives hear from families about how their decisions impact the everyday lives of children and youth with disabilities. Thank you for making your voice heard!

Sincerely,

Paula Goldberg
Executive Director
