Working with Multicultural Families

Friday, June 23, 2017 from 9:00 a.m. to 11:30 a.m.

Location: PACER Center

Families from diverse cultures often have unique experiences when they participate in their child's education. Research shows that when school staff work with families, using culturally competent strategies, it paves the way for meaningful family engagement and better outcomes for students.

This session will help all school professionals gain an understanding of how to engage families of diverse students and provide information about tools that can be used to involve families in their child's education. Multicultural advocates will present and respond to your questions about working with African American, Hmong, Latino and Somali families. Each participant will be given a free packet of tools and resources for working with families from diverse cultures, including a copy of our Family Fun Reading and Math cards in 4 languages!

Please Register by June 16.


This activity was made possible in part through funding from the Minnesota Department of Education. The source of the funds is from the federal award, State Personnel Development Grant.
