A Simon Technology Center workshop

Reality Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC)

Monday, March 19, 2012 from 9am - 3pm

Learn how to integrate any type of augmentative communication device into a classroom. This high-energy, day long workshop is full of video examples to illustrate AAC and linguistic competence, group lessons, literacy, writing, and differentiated learning. Leave with a jump drive full of resources and information for using AAC in the classroom.  Augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) enhances and/or replaces conventional forms of communication and is used by people with disabilities as a method of expressive speech.

  • ASHA CEU's will be provided. 
  • A lunch break provided with lunch on your own.
  • This training focuses on strategies that can be used and incorporated on any AAC system.
  • Although this training is primarily geared toward professionals working in the schools, parents are welcome to attend.

Presenter bio:

Jane Odom, M.Ed. has more than 13 years of teaching experience in the field of Special Education. Jane currently serves as the Educational Segment Manager for PRC and is responsible for focusing on increasing knowledge of the education segment and creating innovative approaches to implement PRC devices and Unity in school settings.

Jane is a member of the International Society for Augmentative and Alternative Communication (ISAAC), holds a Pennsylvania Professional Certificate in Elementary Ed. and Special Ed. and a South Carolina Professional Certificate in Special Ed. Mental Retardation, Special Ed. Orthopedic Handicapped and Driver's Education. Jane also has a membership with the International Society for Technology in Education.
