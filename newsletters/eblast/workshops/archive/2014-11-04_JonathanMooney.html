Don’t Eat the Marshmallow

Thursday, November 13, from 6:30 to 8:30 p.m.

We all want students who have disabilities, such as autism, a learning disability, or ADHD, to thrive and be successful in life, but what does it take? New research shows the importance of resiliency, perseverance, and hope, not just academic skills and cognitive ability. National speaker Jonathan Mooney will bring this topic to life and explore concrete ways parents, professionals, and educators can build these important life skills.

Jonathan Mooney is a person who has dyslexia as well as a writer and activist who learned to read when he was 12 years old. He has since earned an honors degree in English Literature at Brown University and has written and published two books.
