CARE COORDINATION: Empowering Parents

October 29, 2016, from 9:00 AM — 5:30PM at PACER Center

Application Deadline: Oct. 20th - Limited space available!

PACER Center is hosting a free training for parents of children with special health care needs and/or disabilities to learn how to navigate and improve Care Coordination of your child’s medical providers. The goal is to improve the quality of medical care your child receives.

Eligible participants must be:

  • a parent (or legal guardian) of a child from birth to 18 years of age
  • a parent or legal guardian of a child diagnosed or identified as having complex medical health care needs
  • able to commit to participate in a full day of training on October 29, from 9:00AM until 5:30PM

The purpose of the training is to help you:

  • identify a medical home; a clinic or Doctor that offers a family centered, coordinated approach to medical care
  • learn techniques to organize your child’s medical records and information
  • find reliable resources to address your child’s medical needs
  • improve your skills for effective communication and coordination between multiple medical providers
  • plan for your child’s transition through various developmental stages
  • develop skills for coping with stress and finding support
  • build your advocacy skills to improve health outcomes for your child
  • network with other parents

Participants will be chosen from a pool of applicants who meet the criteria, on a first come first serve basis. Preregistration is required. 

If interested, please respond as soon as possible to Linda Goldman Cherwitz or phone (952)838-1350.
