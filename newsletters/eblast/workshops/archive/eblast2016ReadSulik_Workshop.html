Don't miss dynamic national speaker Dr. Read Sulik's two free workshops on Depression and ADHD in children

Helping children and young adults with depression or Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) are two of the most common challenges facing parents and educators today. By popular demand, Dr. Read Sulik returns to PACER for two outstanding workshops. Reserve your seat today!

Dr. L. Read Sulik is a child and adolescent, and adult psychiatrist. He is the Chief Integration Officer at PrairieCare and Executive Director of the newly founded PrairieCare Institute for Care Delivery Innovation, Education, Training and Research in Minneapolis Minnesota, where he leads the organization’s initiatives in integrating behavioral health services into primary care and other settings. Dr. Sulik, who has received numerous national honors and awards, lectures across the country and internationally on children’s mental health issues, the integration of primary and behavioral healthcare, wellness and leadership.

Understanding Depression and How to Help Your Child

Tuesday, March 15, 2016 from 6:30 p.m. to 8:30 p.m.

Parenting a child with mental health challenges can be incredibly difficult at times, but there are skills you can learn to help your child. In this interactive workshop, Dr. Sulik will help parents and professionals learn about "The 3s" of Depression – changes in mood, changes in sleep and energy, and changes in thinking – and the common problems that result. Participants will learn skills that parents can use to help their child heal, recover, and prevent recurrences of depression.

Understanding ADHD in Children and Young Adults: What you can do

Thursday, April 21, 2016 from 6:30 p.m. to 8:30 p.m.

Dr. Sulik describes Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) as the inability of a child to regulate him- or herself. "Children with ADHD can't regulate their attention, their behavior, or their emotions very well," he said, "and often they can't regulate their physical activity, either." Dr. Sulik will provide the latest information on ADHD to help parents of children with the disorder and the professionals who work with them to understand the disorder better and what they can do to help.
