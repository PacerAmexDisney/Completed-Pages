If you are a family leader ready to make a difference in your community while taking your leadership skills to the next level, this is for you!

The Health Information Center’s Family Leadership Institute is for MN parents of children and youth with special health care needs and/or disabilities. Past Leadership Institute participants have given us great reviews! Many of the participants have gone on to provide trainings, support and information to families and professionals in their own communities on navigating and improving MN heath care systems.

Apply Today!

The training includes 2 in-person  sessions, 2 webinar streaming sessions and 1 conference call. Lodging may be provided for the in-person meetings for those traveling more than 70 miles one way.

What past participants have said:

  •  “great opportunity to network and learn from peers from all around the state”
  •  “this training  will not only help my family but I will be able to share accurate info with others in my community to help them access needed services”
  • “accurate and clear information on MN health care financing options“
  • “really helpful info on identifying and understanding my strengths and how to use them effectively as an advocate for my family and community”
  • “thank you for this awesome opportunity!”

Training topics include:

  • Rethinking your role
  • An overview of MN health care financing
  • Effectively telling your story and working with policy makers
  • Mapping  your systems of care and care coordination

Each participant will be expected to complete a project of your choice to share with the other parents going through the program. Project examples might include:

  • Meeting with your state representative or senator to discuss issues families of children and youth with special health care needs are concerned about.
  • Developing a shared care plan with your child’s primary doctor.
  • Organizing a meeting with a small group of other families to share what you have learned in this program with others.
