Teens’ "Votes" Can Help the Teens Against Bullying project at PACER be awarded up to $250,000!

Best Buy’s @15 Change Exchange program has chosen PACER as one of four organizations to receive a charitable donation. Teens’ votes determine what percentage of $250,000 PACER will receive to support its National Bullying Prevention Center and its new Web site, PACERTeensAgainstBullying.org.

Here’s how it works: Teens age 14 to 18 can go to at15.com, where they register and earn points when they play games, take surveys, post a comment on the discussion forum, or take part in the site in other ways. The more they do on the site, the more points they earn.
During the last two weeks of October, they can click on the “@15 Change Exchange” link to give their points to Teens Against Bullying.
When teens vote for PACER, they:

  • Empower teens everywhere to stand up to bullying
  • Create the momentum that builds a better world
  • Make their voice heard around the nation

Tell teens you know to sign up now and pass the word! Teens can earn more points for PACER when they refer a friend to the site.
