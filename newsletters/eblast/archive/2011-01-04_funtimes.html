Exciting Opportunity for High School students and Young Adults with Disabilities!
Participate in Four Adaptive Ski Lessons Starting Monday, January 24th

PACER Fun Times is a volunteer, social program that connects high school teens and young adults with and without disabilities. Social events, such as movies, sporting events, pizza parties are scheduled every other month during the school year in the Twin Cities metro area.

Fun Times has an exciting opportunity coming up to participate in a series of four adaptive ski lessons starting Monday, Jan. 24, and running consecutively on Jan. 31, Feb. 7 and Feb. 14, from 7 to 9 p.m., at Buck Hill in Burnsville.

This event is a partnership between PACER Fun Times and Rolf Tengdin, a 16-year-old Edina High School student working toward the completion of his Eagle Scout Project. Together, our goal is to give students with disabilities an opportunity to "Learn to Ski.” Collaborating on this project are: Jeff Kennedy, a nationally known expert on adaptive skiing, Special Kid Care, the Edina High School Ski Team and the team’s head coach, Kevin Shields. Buck Hill adaptive ski instructors will provide direct instruction at these lessons. PACER Fun Times volunteers and Edina High School Ski Team members will also provide additional support.

This event is for high school age students and young adults with disabilities.

Deadline for preliminary registration is Jan. 13, 2011. Space is limited.

To sign up for this event or request more information, email funtimes@pacer.org or call 952-838-9000.

To learn more about PACER's Fun Times Project go to http://www.pacer.org/funtimes.
