Early Childhood Workshops

Free PACER workshops for parents of children with disabilities from birth to age 5.

Families Are Important

Understanding the Early Intervention Process in Minnesota

Saturday, Sept. 26, 2009 – 9:30 to 11:30 a.m. see the flyer

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) recognizes the important role families play in their children’s lives. Families know their child’s strengths, interests, and frustrations, and so they must be included in the evaluation process and in developing outcomes for their child. This free workshop will help parents understand the early intervention process in Minnesota. Register online

Building Blocks of Early Literacy

Monday, Oct. 5, 2009 – 6:30 to 9 p.m. see the flyer

A free workshop for parents of children from birth through age 5 with developmental delays or disabilities.

Early literacy skills are the foundation for developing language and reading skills. They’re also essential for school success.

Whatever your young child’s level of ability, you can help promote those important skills through everyday activities. Register online

Understanding and Addressing Challenging Behaviors in Your Young Child

Tuesday, Oct. 20, 2009 – 6:30 to 8:30 p.m. see the flyer

This free workshop is an opportunity for families of children with disabilities from birth to age 5 to obtain vital information that can make a difference in their child’s life. Register online
