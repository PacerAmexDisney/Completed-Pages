PACER Legislative Alert - Supporting Youth With Disabilities

The U.S. Congress is considering changes to the Workforce Investment Act and the federal Vocational Rehabilitation (VR) Program that assists youth and adults with disabilities to get the education and training they need to obtain and retain a job. While Congress is trying to strengthen the VR services that students with disabilities would receive as they transition out of high school, we are concerned that some possible changes would hurt families of youth with disabilities. PACER is asking families and others to call their representatives about the following points critical to families and youth:

  1. Oppose moving the Vocational Rehabilitation and Assistive Technology programs out of the Department of Education. The Senate version of this legislation seeks to move VR to the Department of Labor and the Assistive Technology program to the Department of Health and Human Services. Moving these programs out of the Department of Education will disconnect them from a culture valuing parental involvement and the other programs that support students with disabilities.  We are concerned such a move will only damage strong family connections and important services provided to youth and adults with disabilities here in Minnesota.

  2. Strengthen competitive employment awareness provisions. Ensuring that young adults with disabilities transitioning from high school to employment are aware that jobs which pay the minimum wage and higher are available is an important aspect of providing individuals the opportunity to reach their potential. The bill could be strengthened by ensuring that there are no exceptions to making sure young adults are made aware of their opportunity to work in a competitive workplace setting before selecting a non-competitive, often less than minimum wage, work experience.

Please contact your U.S. Representative today about these important issues. Find their phone number here: http://house.gov/representatives/find/.
