Please help Children, especially children with challenging behavior! Please Call Congressman John Kline Today!

Please Call Congressman John Kline, Ranking Republican, Education and Labor Committee, and let him know that you support H.R. 4247, which prevents and reduces the use of restraint and seclusion in schools for ALL children.  Seclusion and restraint are procedures that are used by some school staff, often for challenging behavior, and include pinning children to the ground, strapping or tying them into chairs, and isolating them in separate, locked rooms for periods of time.  These procedures are not regulated in our public schools and often end in injury or in some cases death for a child.

A critical vote will be made in the Education and Labor Committee, this Thursday, February 4th.

Due to his position in the Education and Labor Committee, we need to get Congressman Kline’s support for his bill before February 4th.

Here’s What You Need to Do:

  1. Contact Congressman Kline’s local office at (952) 808-1213.  They will direct your call to the appropriate person. 

    OR

    Contact the DC office at (202) 225-2271.  Ask to speak to Molly Conway,
    Education Legislative Assistant for Congressman Kline.  After you leave your call, you can also follow-up with an email to her at Molly.Conway@mail.house.gov
  2. Be sure to tell Congressman Kline’s office that you are a constituent of his and that you SUPPORT H.R. 4247, to prevent and reduce the use of restraint and seclusion;  You encourage Congressman Kline to support it as well in committee on Thursday, February 4th.    Ask if you can count on his support.
  3. If your child has experienced seclusion and restraint, be sure to let them know what your child’s disability is and the impact of these procedures on your child.  Keep it brief!

Here’s Some Key Strengths of the Bill (you can mention that you support): 

  • H.R. 4247 protects ALL children and will help reduce and prevent these procedures in schools.  Children with disabilities should be protected under this legislation.  Schools should not be allowed to substitute writing these procedures into Individual Education Programs (IEP) to avoid including children with disabilities being protected under this legislation.
  • Schools will be required to notify parents within 24 hours when these procedures are used.
  • Schools cannot use restraints that restrict breathing.
  • Strong behavioral intervention support components are included.
  • Adequate training of staff is required.
  • Data and reporting for use of these procedures is required.
