Minnesota Safe and Supportive Schools Conference: Addressing Bullying in Schools

This one-day conference, presented by the Minnesota Department of Education School Safety Technical Assistance Center in partnership with PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center and Hamline University, is designed for Minnesota school administrators, educators, school support staff, parents, student leaders, education stakeholders and community members to help participants build on their knowledge about bullying prevention and creating positive school climates.

  • Date and Time: Friday, January 22, 2016 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.
  • Location: Hamline University in St. Paul
  • Cost: $20 for early registration and $30 after January 4, 2016.

Fee includes CEU’s, refreshments, and lunch

Register now at PACER.org/bullying!

Agenda includes:

  • Morning Keynote: “The Challenges, Emerging Issues, and Promising Practices in Improving School Climate” presented by David Esquith, Director, U.S. Department of Education Office of Safe and Healthy Students Director, Washington D.C.
  • Afternoon Keynote: “Creating Safe and Supportive Schools,” presented by David Osher, Vice President, American Institutes of Research, Washington D.C.

Topics include:

  • Working with children and adolescents who bully.
  • Supporting students in bullying situations with practical solutions.
  • Being safe on social media, including "social reporting" presented by Facebook.
  • Addressing gender identity-based bullying.
  • Bullying involving students with disabilities.
  • Addressing the biases behind bullying.
  • Examining restorative practices in schools.
  • Using the Minnesota School Survey data to create youth profiles of developmental skills, supports and challenges.
