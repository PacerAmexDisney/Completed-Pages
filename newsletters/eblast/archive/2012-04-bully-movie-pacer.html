Reality check: “Bully” movie is a call to action

Support Work of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center Today!

America is waking up to the harsh realities of bullying thanks to PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center and the new documentary “Bully.” As a partner in the Bully Project, PACER is providing educators, students, families, and individuals with the resources to address bullying but demand is growing rapidly and we need your help.

For too long, our culture has accepted bullying as a normal part of growing up. “Bully” gives everyone a heartbreaking look at the reality of bullying and we believe the movie will be a call to action. Together, we can help create a climate that no longer accepts this behavior.

The “New York Times” says the film documents the emergence of a movement and a “shift in consciousness of the kind that occurs when isolated, oppressed individuals discover that they are not alone.”

“USA Today” calls it “insightful” and “moving” and says the film “forces audiences to face actions that are unthinkable, inexcusable, and excruciatingly sad.”



The end of bullying begins with you!

More than 160,000 students in America stayed home from school today because of threats and intimidation from other students. Help them feel safe by supporting the work of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center.

If you have experienced the pain of bullying in your life, or the lives of your children, then you know how devastating it can be. If not, then make the decision to stand up, protect others, and show your support for this important cause.  Please do what you can to help.

  • See the film yourself – “Bully” opens nationwide on April 13
  • Tell your friends to see the film – Use Facebook and Twitter to spread the word
  • Utilize PACER’s bullying prevention tools – available at PACER.org/bullying
  • Support the work of PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center – DONATE today

 

Thank you for your support!
