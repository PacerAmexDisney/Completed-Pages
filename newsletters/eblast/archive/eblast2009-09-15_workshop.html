Computer Skills and Resources
to Close the Digital Divide

These free workshops are for parents who have children with disabilities and
who are computer beginners or have limited access to technology. Come explore how
computers and the Internet can benefit your child.

Computer and Assistive Technology Basics

Thursday, Sept. 17, 2009, 1 to 2:30 p.m.

Come learn the basic skills needed to use a computer and navigate the Internet. You’ll also find out what assistive technology is, how it can benefit people with disabilities, and how you can have access to computers and the Internet for little or no money. Register online

Free/Open Source Software and Internet Resources

Wednesday, Sept. 23, 2009, 1 to 2:30 p.m.

Learn about the many free software and Internet resources available for writing, e-mailing, translating, sharing photos, and more. Discover where to find them and how to make them work for you. Register online

How to Access, Download, and Play Free Digital Books for Children

Thursday, Oct. 1, 2009, 10 to 11:30 a.m.

The Internet offers a wealth of free books to help children read, learn, and grow. Come discover where to find them and how to share them. Register online
