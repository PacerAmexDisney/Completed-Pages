PACER Day at the Capitol:  “Be a Champion!”

When:

Thursday, March 13, 2014 at 9:30 a.m. 
Begin your week as a Champion for Children! PACER chose this day because it begins one of the most important weeks for legislators to hear policy bills and make decisions on which bills will pass through Committee.

What:

Join us for PACER Day at the Capitol and together we will have our voices heard. Minnesota legislators are smart, hard-working public servants, but at the end of the day they are people who often make decisions based on real stories from their constituents. We will provide training so that you can effectively tell your story and impact the political process that is so important to the hope and success of all our children.

Where:

Veterans Services Building, 5th Floor Conference Room
Located at the south end of the State Capitol lawn (20 12th St. W., St. Paul)

Schedule:

9:30 a.m.

 Check-in and Registration


10 a.m. to noon

Participate in a dynamic workshop that includes a panel discussion with advocacy and education experts, state legislators, and one of the most influential lobbyists for children with disabilities. Hear their perspectives on the hottest issues and bills relating to our children, including special education, mental health, education funding, limiting the use of seclusion and restraint in schools, and bullying prevention that will impact Minnesota children for generations to come!

Learn to tell your personal story to influence these important issues by writing, calling, and meeting with your legislators. Share your journey and ask for their help in providing hope and opportunities for the success of all children with disabilities.


Noon to 1 p.m.

Pizza, prepare and practice talking to our legislators.


1 p.m.

 Meet with your legislators and make a difference!

Register:

Register here or call (952) 838-9000.

Please provide your name, contact information, and the number and names of people in your group. Space is limited.

If you register by March 7, PACER will provide you with contact information for your legislators so you can make appointments in advance to meet with them at the Capitol on March 13.
