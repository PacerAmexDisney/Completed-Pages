What is Project KITE?

Project KITE is a series of five FREE workshops that travels to your location! These workshops will train and teach you and your child’s teachers how to use and consider assistive technologies to help break down barriers to learning.

More Information >>>

Why Should I Participate in Project KITE?

  • Assistive technologies can open doors to new skills and abilities for a child with disabilities, and Project KITE is a great way to explore them!
  • Project KITE includes the loan of an iPad with hundreds of useful learning apps for the duration of the program, FREE membership to our assistive technology lending library, and useful technology trainings with a PACER assistive technology specialist.
  • Upon successful attendance and completion of Project KITE your child’s classroom will receive a FREE Young Explorer Computer (pictured above) valued at over $2,500.
  • A team is built around a focus child (aged 3-8) that includes: a parent, a teacher and a related service provider (for example: Speech Therapist, Paraprofessional, Occupational Therapist, etc). All members must apply.
  • Three separate teams make a complete application. Individual teams do not need to be from the same school, but must be from a similar geographic location.

 

To learn more about KITE or to apply visit: www.pacer.org/stc/kite.  Interested parents should contact Project KITE Coordinator Terri Rosen at terri.rosen@pacer.org or 952-838-9000.

Applications are due by Friday, February 6th, 2015.
