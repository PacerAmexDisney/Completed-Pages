What is Project KITE?

Project KITE is a series of five free workshops. These workshops pair the parents of children with disabilities with their child's classroom teacher and related service providers as they learn about different assistive technologies designed to help their child, student and classroom thrive.

More Information >>>

Why Should I Participate in Project KITE?

Assistive technology can be as simple as a pencil grip or as complex as an iPad, and when used effectively can make a world of difference for young students with disabilities. During Project KITE, parents and professionals learn and work together to discover and use assistive technologies that benefit their child, student and classroom. Project KITE includes iPad app training and incorporates the newest technologies available. Teachers and service providers completing the training will receive clock hours for their participation.  Upon completion of Project KITE, each participating team will receive a FREE Young Explorer Computer (pictured above) valued at over $2,500.

Three teams, with three people per team are needed to make a registered team. A single team consists of:

  • a parent of a child with special needs
  • the classroom teacher
  • one other related service provider working with the child(for example, Speech Therapist, Occupational Therapist and others)

Interested parents and professionals should contact John at stc@pacer.org or (800) 537-2237. For more information and to submit an application, please visit www.pacer.org/stc/kite.

Applications are due by Wednesday, February 06, 2013.
