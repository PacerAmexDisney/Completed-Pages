What is Project KITE?

Project KITE is a series of five FREE workshops that travels to your location! These workshops bring together education professionals and the parents of the children they work with to help them learn about assistive technologies designed to help children learn.

More Information >>>

Why Should I Participate in Project KITE?

Assistive technology can be as simple as a pencil grip or as complex as an iPad, and when used effectively can make a world of difference for young students with disabilities. During Project KITE, parents and professionals learn and work together to discover and use assistive technologies that benefit their child, your student and the classroom. Project KITE includes iPad app training and incorporates the newest technologies available. Teachers and service providers completing the training will receive clock hours for their participation. Upon completion of Project KITE, each participating team will receive a FREE Young Explorer Computer (pictured above) valued at over $2,500 for the classroom.

A single team is built around a focus child (aged 3-8) and consists of:

  • a parent of a child with special needs
  • a classroom teacher
  • a related service provider working with the child (for example: Special Education Teacher, Paraprofessional, Occupational Therapist etc.)
