Project KITE workshop series now has open registration.

Don’t miss this unique opportunity to participate in a special five-part Project KITE workshop series.

Project KITE is a training curriculum for parents and teachers designed to promote inclusion and independence of young children with disabilities through the use of assistive technology. In this action-oriented series for parents and educators, you’ll learn how to discuss, identify, and implement assistive technology to benefit your child, student, or classroom.

What’s a Team?

A team consists of three people including:

  • Parent of a child with a disability (ages 3 to 8)
  • The child’s teacher
  • A related service provider working with the child, such as an occupational therapist, speech language pathologist, or paraprofessional

How to Register

Register here for each of the Project KITE Series of workshops.

If you are part of a team, use the “Additional Information” box to type “Team” followed by the child’s first and last name, and the city and state of the child’s school. For example:  “Team John Smith, Bloomington, MN.”  Once we have received registrations from a parent, teacher, and service provider for that student you will be qualified for the KITE Team benefits and we will contact you to gather some additional required information.

Participate Online via Livestream

Can’t make it to a workshop in person? Individuals and teams can participate online via Livestream! Just select the “online via web” option when registering. The URL to attend the Livestream event will be sent to all registrants at least 1 week prior to the workshop.
