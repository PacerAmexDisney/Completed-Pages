What is Project KITE?

Project KITE is a series of five free workshops that travels to your Minnesota location! These workshops will train and teach you and your child’s educational team how to use and consider assistive technology to help break down barriers to learning.

Why Should I Participate in Project KITE?

Assistive technology can be as simple as a pencil grip or as complex as an iPad, and when used effectively can make a world of difference for young students with disabilities. During Project KITE, parents and professionals learn and work together to discover and use assistive technology that benefit their child, your student and the classroom. Project KITE includes iPad app training and incorporates a broad array of available technology. Teachers and service providers completing the training will receive clock hours for their participation.

Three separate teams with three members per team are needed to make a complete application. Teams must be from a similar geographic area.

Each single team is built around a focus child (aged 3-8) and consists of:

  • a parent of a child with special needs
  • a classroom teacher
  • a related service provider working with the child (for example: Special Education Teacher, Speech Therapist, Paraprofessional, Occupational Therapist etc.)

To learn more about KITE and to apply visit: www.pacer.org/stc/kite. Questions may be directed to Project KITE Coordinator Terri Rosen — terri.rosen@pacer.org or 952-838-9000.
