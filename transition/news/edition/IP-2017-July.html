Recent resources highlighting strategies to improve transition planning and post school outcomes for culturally diverse youth are the focus of this issue of NPCTE’s Inspiring Possibilities. View Online.

Transition Planning Tips for Linguistically and Culturally Diverse Youth with Disabilities

This new PACER publication offers several culturally appropriate practices to use with youth and families as they transition from high school to higher education, employment, and independent living. Read more (pdf).

Decreasing the Dropout Rate for Young Men with Disabilities from Culturally and Ethnically Diverse Backgrounds

A new resource from the National Technical Assistance Center on Transition (NTACT) on school completion highlights family engagement, school policy, and early warning systems strategies that schools can use to decrease the dropout rate of minority male youth with disabilities. Read more (pdf).

Getting Off to a Good Start: Positive Interactions with Diverse Families

This PACER publication offers tips school professionals can use to work effectively with culturally diverse youth of all ages (not just transition-age youth) and their families. Read more (pdf).

Research on Parent Involvement with Diverse Families

A list of recent research literature on multicultural parent involvement and engagement was developed by PACER staff to help address the needs of educators who work with diverse youth and their families. It is periodically updated. Read more.

Best Practices in Outreach

The new Center for Parent Information and Resources webpage highlights several publications on the topic of outreach to diverse cultural communities that were reviewed and recommended by a team of parent center directors and coordinators. Read more.

NCWD/Youth Publications in Spanish/Español

Several publications developed by the National Collaborative on Workforce and Disability for Youth (NCWD/Youth) for youth, families, professionals and employers are available in Spanish. Check them out here.

PACER’s National Parent Center on Transition and Employment® provides quality information and resources for families of young adults with disabilities on transition planning, civil rights, work-based learning, career accommodations, higher education, financial aid, and much more! Whether just starting the transition planning process or searching for information on a specific topic, PACER.org/transition is a comprehensive source of information designed to support families’ varied needs.
